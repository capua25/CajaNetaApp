1. Basic Components and Ratios

Unit Contribution Margin (mc):
mc = p−cv

Explanation: This is the difference between the unit selling price (p) and the unit variable cost (cv). It represents the specific benefit obtained from producing and selling one additional unit.

Contribution Ratio (rc): 
rc = p/mc
 
Explanation: This measures the relationship between the contribution margin and the selling price. It indicates the amount that each peso sold contributes toward covering fixed costs and generating profit.

Variable Cost Ratio (rcv): 
rcv = cv/p

Explanation: This represents the investment in variable costs for every peso of sales.

Complementary Relationship: 
rc+rcv=1

Explanation: The sum of the contribution ratio and the variable cost ratio always equals 1 (or 100% of the sale price).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. The Equilibrium Point (Point of Indifference)

The point of equilibrium is the sales level where total revenues equal total costs, resulting in zero profit.

In Units (Qe): 
Qe = CF/mc
 
Explanation: This formula determines the number of units that must be sold so that the total contribution margin exactly covers the fixed costs (CF).

In Monetary Value (Ve): 
Ve= CF/rc
 
Explanation: This calculates the total sales revenue in pesos required to reach the equilibrium point.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. Multi-Product Analysis

When a company sells more than one product, it must use the sales mix to find the equilibrium point.

Weighted Margin of the Mix (mc~mix~): 
mc~mix~ = ∑(mc~i~ × %units~i~)

Explanation: To calculate the equilibrium in units for a mix, you multiply each product's individual contribution margin by its percentage share of the total units sold.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4. Financial and Safety Metrics

Financial Equilibrium Point: 
PE~financial~ = CF~erogables~/rc

Explanation: This determines the minimum sales volume needed to cover erodible fixed costs (those requiring actual cash outlays, like rent or salaries, excluding non-cash items like depreciation). It marks the volume needed to avoid a financial deficit.

Margin of Safety (MS): 

MS = (V − V~equilibrio~)/V
 
Explanation: This is expressed as a percentage and shows how much sales can drop before the company begins to lose money. A higher margin of safety indicates a lower risk of incurring losses.

Summary of Nomenclature:
V: Total Sales
p: Unit price
CF: Fixed Costs
CV: Total Variable Costs
cv: Unit variable cost
Q: Total quantity sold
